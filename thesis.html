§ 06 - Thesis

Small systems,
kept useful.

Flits exists to build and hold practical software, quiet assets, and young companies with enough patience to let them compound.

A standing note on how we decide what belongs here.

What we believe

Software companies do not all need to become venture-scale stories. Some should stay small, useful, profitable, and cared for over a long period of time.

Flits is a home for those companies. We build some from scratch, acquire or back others, and keep a small inventory of digital assets that may become useful later.

What we keep

We are interested in products with a clear job, a small but real audience, and enough restraint to avoid becoming bloated. We prefer boring revenue, durable names, simple operations, and teams that know exactly who they serve.

We are not trying to assemble a fund, chase categories, or manufacture urgency. We would rather own fewer things well than collect many things loosely.

How we work

We move slowly by design. We write things down, keep overhead low, and make decisions that still make sense if nobody is watching.

The best Flits company should feel independent, useful, and slightly inevitable: small enough to understand, strong enough to last.
